# Chapter 20 — Master Panel Procedures — All Coverage Zones


Complete install procedures for every major PPF coverage zone. Each procedure includes pre-install checklist, step sequence, QC points, and defect reference.



### Hood Panel — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** ★★★☆☆ Intermediate · **Typical time:** 90–150 min · **Film orientation:** Pattern aligned to factory body lines


The hood is the highest-visibility PPF panel and the foundation of every partial-front package. Success depends on clean anchor placement, controlled slip, and post-heat along crown lines.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Document pre-existing chips and scratches with photos</li>
<li>Complete full wash, clay, iron removal, and IPA wipe</li>
<li>Confirm paint correction is complete before pattern placement</li>
<li>Stage hood in shade; surface temp below 95°F</li>
<li>Lay pattern on clean surface; verify orientation and overlap marks</li>
<li>Mix fresh slip solution; stage heat gun and multiple squeegee durometers</li>
<li>Mask wiper cowl and leading edge if customer requests</li>
<li>Verify plotter pattern includes mirror of OEM badge cutouts if applicable</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Hood Panel Install Sequence

1. Position pattern on hood; tape hinge side as anchor reference
2. Peel 6-inch release strip along top edge; mist adhesive with slip
3. Lay top edge; align centerline to hood crease
4. Squeegee anchor band across top with firm overlapping passes
5. Peel liner in 12-inch sections while partner mists adhesive
6. Work from center outward using wet squeegee technique
7. Relieve tension at crown with light heat and micro-adjustments
8. Address light fingers at corners with localized heat and stretch
9. Wrap leading edge under lip using soft squeegee and heat assist
10. Tuck film into seam at fender junction without bridging
11. Trim excess at perimeter with fresh blade and light pressure
12. Post-heat entire panel 200–220°F moving continuously
13. Inspect optically for silvering, contamination, and edge lift

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No trapped contamination under film</li>
<li>Edges wrapped or tucked per shop standard</li>
<li>No silvering or adhesive starvation visible</li>
<li>Leading edge sealed with no water channels</li>
<li>Post-heat completed on all stretched zones</li>
<li>Customer pre-damage documented separately</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Anchor the flat crown first. Stretch only where geometry demands it.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Never bridge a body seam with tension. Fresh paint under 30 days must not be wrapped.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Silvering at crown | Insufficient slip or premature squeegee pressure | Lift film, remist, reapply with lighter passes | Maintain wet squeegee path |
| Finger at leading edge | Film stretched before anchor set | Heat and relax; re-anchor from center | Set anchor band before any stretch |
| Edge lift at fender line | Incomplete tuck or trapped solution | Lift edge, dry channel, re-tuck with heat | Post-dry seam before final tuck |



### Front Bumper Cover — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** ★★★★☆ Advanced · **Typical time:** 2–4 hours · **Film orientation:** Multiple pieces; bumper off-vehicle preferred


Modern bumper covers combine deep recesses, sensor pockets, and compound curves. Bumper-off installation dramatically improves edge quality and reduces rework.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Remove bumper per OEM service procedure when possible</li>
<li>Clean backside channels and fog light pockets</li>
<li>Identify ADAS sensor zones; use sensor-safe film if required</li>
<li>Template or plot all pieces before adhesive contact</li>
<li>Stage heat gun, relief cut tools, and soft squeegees</li>
<li>Verify paint is fully cured on repainted bumpers</li>
<li>Photograph parking sensors and trim positions</li>
<li>Lay pieces in install order: center lower, upper, sides</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Front Bumper Cover Install Sequence

1. Install center lower piece first as primary anchor
2. Work upward in overlapping sections per pattern guide
3. Heat and relief-cut only where pattern indicates
4. Set fog light and grille surround pieces before outer corners
5. Use foam squeegee in deep recesses; hard card on flat zones
6. Stretch into pockets with heat; never cold-stretch TPU
7. Tuck all perimeter edges into factory seams
8. Trim at invisible lines behind bumper lip
9. Reinstall bumper on vehicle for final edge check
10. Align film seams to factory panel gaps
11. Post-heat all compound curves and recessed areas
12. Final QC with cross-light at multiple angles

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No lifted edges at grille surround</li>
<li>Sensor areas clear and film not bridging vents</li>
<li>Seams aligned to factory gaps</li>
<li>No contamination in recesses</li>
<li>Post-heat completed on all stretched areas</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Bumper-off saves hours on complex covers. Label every piece before removal.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Do not cover active sensor faces unless using approved sensor film.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bridging at recess | Insufficient relief or heat | Add relief cut; heat and reset film | Follow pattern relief marks |
| Wrinkle at corner | Cold stretch or trapped air | Lift, heat, reapply with slip | Heat before stretch; work wet |



### Front Fender (Full) — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** ★★★☆☆ Intermediate · **Typical time:** 60–90 min · **Film orientation:** Vertical install; top edge at fender line


Fender panels introduce vertical orientation and wheel-well proximity. Wheel liner protection and clean upper edge alignment separate professional installs from average work.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Mask wheel opening if installing with wheel on</li>
<li>IPA wipe full fender including behind handle area</li>
<li>Confirm pattern accounts for antenna or side marker</li>
<li>Stage film vertically to reduce contamination drop-in</li>
<li>Protect adjacent door edge with tape if partially open</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Front Fender (Full) Install Sequence

1. Anchor along fender upper character line
2. Squeegee downward in overlapping vertical passes
3. Wrap forward edge into door jamb zone where applicable
4. Heat and set around wheel arch flare
5. Tuck trailing edge at door gap
6. Trim at factory seam lines
7. Post-heat along arch and upper curve
8. Inspect from multiple angles with inspection light

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Upper edge parallel to body line</li>
<li>Wheel arch clean with no fingers</li>
<li>Door gap edge uniform</li>
<li>No contamination visible</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Work top-down on vertical panels to control contamination.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Watch for fresh touch-up paint near wheel impact zones.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Finger at wheel arch | Insufficient heat on compound curve | Heat and micro-stretch while wet | Pre-heat arch zone before laydown |



### Mirror Cap — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** ★★★★☆ Advanced · **Typical time:** 30–45 min per cap · **Film orientation:** Small compound curve; pre-stretch recommended


Mirror caps are high-impact zones with tight compound curves. Pre-stretching on a flat table before application prevents adhesive starvation on the crown.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Remove cap if shop procedure allows</li>
<li>Clean with IPA; inspect for prior coating</li>
<li>Cut pattern with extra bleed on all sides</li>
<li>Pre-stretch on table with heat to 10–15% elongation</li>
<li>Allow film to relax 30 seconds before application</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Mirror Cap Install Sequence

1. Mist cap and adhesive generously
2. Position pre-stretched film on cap crown
3. Squeegee from center outward with soft tool
4. Work into undercut with heat assist
5. Trim at hidden underside edge
6. Post-heat entire cap surface
7. Reinstall and verify no edge lift at seam

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No silvering on crown</li>
<li>Underside edge hidden from view</li>
<li>Secure adhesion on undercut</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Pre-stretch saves mirror caps. Never install cold on deep crowns.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Heated mirror glass may affect adhesive — allow cooling before install.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Crown silvering | Cold install on compound curve | Remove and reapply with pre-stretch | Always pre-stretch caps |



### Headlight / Fog Light — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** ★★★☆☆ Intermediate · **Typical time:** 20–40 min per unit · **Film orientation:** Custom template or plotter piece


Headlight protection preserves clarity and prevents rock pitting. Use headlight-specific film thickness and verify legal visibility requirements in your market.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Clean lens with plastic-safe cleaner only</li>
<li>Clay bar lens if bonded contamination present</li>
<li>Mask surrounding paint if trimming in place</li>
<li>Confirm film is optically clear and UV stable</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Headlight / Fog Light Install Sequence

1. Dry-fit template on lens
2. Mist lens and adhesive
3. Apply from center outward
4. Squeegee with felt-edge tool
5. Trim at lens perimeter with light blade pressure
6. Heat-set edges lightly
7. Verify beam pattern unaffected

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No haze or distortion</li>
<li>Edges fully adhered</li>
<li>No moisture trapped</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Use softer squeegee on polycarbonate lenses.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Some OEM coatings react to adhesives — test on inconspicuous area.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Haze under film | Contamination or wrong film type | Lift and reapply on clean lens | Use lens-rated film only |

### Rocker Panel & Lower Door — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** ★★★☆☆ Intermediate · **Typical time:** 45–60 min per side · **Film orientation:** Horizontal; high gravel impact zone

### Full Front Capstone Package — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** ★★★★★ Master · **Typical time:** 6–10 hours · **Film orientation:** Multi-panel coordinated install


The full front capstone integrates hood, fenders, bumper, mirrors, headlights, and A-pillars into one seamless protection package. This is the shop signature deliverable.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Complete vehicle intake and full documentation</li>
<li>Confirm all panels corrected and decontaminated</li>
<li>Plot entire package; label every piece</li>
<li>Assign lead installer and assistant roles</li>
<li>Stage vehicle in climate-controlled bay overnight if possible</li>
<li>Verify customer package matches written work order</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Full Front Capstone Package Install Sequence

1. Install order: bumper pieces, hood, fenders, mirrors, pillars, lights
2. Maintain consistent slip technique across all panels
3. Align all seams to factory panel gaps
4. Complete post-heat on every panel before moving to next
5. Final walkaround with cross-light and customer-visible angles
6. Document completion photos for warranty file
7. Deliver with written aftercare and cure instructions

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>All package panels installed per work order</li>
<li>Seams consistent and invisible at normal viewing distance</li>
<li>No contamination across entire front</li>
<li>Post-heat log completed</li>
<li>Customer sign-off on package scope</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Capstone installs are your portfolio piece. Never rush the final panel to finish early.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

If any panel fails QC, stop and correct before continuing — seam alignment compounds across panels.

</div>

### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Visible seam mismatch at fender-hood | Panels installed out of order | Realign at junction before final post-heat | Follow install sequence strictly |
| Customer dispute on coverage | Scope not documented | Reference signed work order and intake photos | Document package scope before keys are received |
