# Chapter 7 — Measuring & Pattern Cutting

Accurate measurement and efficient cutting are core skills that separate experienced installers from beginners. Over-cutting wastes material; under-cutting means re-cutting. This chapter provides measurement standards for every panel type and cutting technique guidance for all scenarios.

## Measurement Standards — Overage by Panel Type

| Panel | Measurement Method | Standard Overage | Reason for Overage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hood | Measure at widest point + longest dimension | 10 cm all sides | Wrap-under on front; fold-over on sides; adjustment at rear |
| Roof | Width at widest + length front-to-rear | 12 cm all sides | Large panel; large correction needed if misaligned |
| Door | Panel face width × height | 8 cm all sides | Jamb fold on all four edges |
| Bumper (sectional) | Per piece — measure each section individually | 10 cm all edges of each section | Multiple pieces; each requires fold or termination overage |
| Mirror cap | Circumference + height | 5 cm all sides | Small piece; compound curves consume more film than expected |
| Pillar | Width × height | 15 mm glass side; 10 mm outer edge | Glass channel tuck; outer seam fold |
| Fender | Width + height including arch | 10 cm; +15 cm on wheel arch side | Arch fold is the most film-consuming edge |
| Quarter panel | Width × height | 15 cm all sides | Complex panel; errors are expensive; generous overage is cheap insurance |

## Cutting Techniques

### Knifeless Tape Method — Preferred for Visible Seams

<div class="procedure">

### Knifeless Tape Cut

1. Apply the knifeless tape to the paint surface along the desired cut line before applying film
2. Run the tape in a single continuous stroke — joins in the tape create visible seam interruptions
3. Apply the film over the top of the tape normally
4. Once the film is adhered, locate the tape pull tab at the end of the run
5. Pull the tape at 45° to the surface in a continuous, controlled motion — do not pull at 90° (tears) or 0° (drags)
6. The monofilament cuts through the adhesive and face film cleanly as it is pulled
7. Post-heat the cut edge and squeegee firmly; apply edge sealer to the cut edge

</div>

### Channel / On-Car Blade Cut Method

<div class="procedure">

### On-Car Blade Cut

1. Use only a specialized channel cutter or a film-safe blade with a rounded tip
2. Hold the blade at 20° to the surface — shallower angles ride on the film, not dig into paint
3. Cut with the blade moving toward you in a pulling motion — not a pushing stroke
4. Use a single pass — multiple passes increase paint-contact risk
5. Immediately remove the trimmed film strip; verify paint is undamaged
6. Post-heat and squeegee the cut edge; seal with edge sealer

</div>

<div class="callout warning">

Channel cutters and 20° blades still have the potential to scratch paint, especially on soft or freshly repainted surfaces. Never drag the blade along paint without the film as a protective layer between blade and surface. When in doubt, use knifeless tape.

</div>
