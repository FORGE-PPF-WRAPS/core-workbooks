# Chapter 9 — Flat Panel Installation

Flat panel installation is the foundational skill upon which all advanced wrap techniques are built. Mastery of the squeegee technique, slip solution management, and post-heat on flat surfaces must be demonstrated before moving to compound curves and complex panels.

## Squeegee Technique Fundamentals

| Variable | Correct Technique | Common Error | Result of Error |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Angle | 45–60° from the panel surface | Perpendicular (90°) | Creates pressure marks; pushes air under film |
| Direction | Center outward in overlapping strokes | Edge-to-edge single pass | Air trapped in center of panel |
| Pressure | Firm and consistent — blade flexes slightly | Excessive pressure | Squeegee scratches or stretch marks on face film |
| Speed | Slow, deliberate — 5–8 cm per second | Fast or erratic | Film shifts on slip; uneven pressure; missed adhesion |
| Overlap | Each stroke overlaps previous by 50% | Single-pass coverage | Missed adhesion bands; bubbles between strokes |

## Air Management Principles

The squeegee technique exists to direct air out of the panel — not just to press the film down. Think of it as sweeping air toward the nearest edge, then completely out. Air that is squeegeed to the edge but not squeegee past the edge creates an edge bubble.

### Flat Panel Installation Sequence

1. Position film over panel with slip solution; verify alignment on all edges
2. Peel liner from center back; fold liner outward on both sides
3. Make one center stroke from top to bottom to anchor the film and prevent movement
4. Working from the center outward: make overlapping horizontal strokes at 45°; each stroke pushes air toward the near edge
5. When a stroke reaches the film edge, lift the squeegee and return to center — do not sweep along the edge
6. After all horizontal strokes, make final vertical strokes from top to bottom to clear remaining air
7. Check under a raking light for remaining bubbles; work them to the edge with targeted squeegee strokes
8. Trim excess film with knifeless tape or a 20° blade
9. Post-heat at 60 °C; make final squeegee pass while film is warm
10. Seal all cut edges with edge sealer
