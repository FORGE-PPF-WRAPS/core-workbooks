# Chapter 167 — Supplemental Panel Procedure Library


Complete panel and zone procedures for advanced coverage areas.



### Hood — Full — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Typical time:** 90–120 min · **Film orientation:** Film grain (if directional) runs front-to-back


The hood is the largest single panel on most vehicles and the most visually prominent. Success requires managing the large film sheet without premature adhesion, keeping the leading edge tension-free while wrapping to the underside, and controlling the side edges around the hood's compound curve drop.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Wash with panel prep soap; clay bar entire surface</li>
<li>IPA wipe to 70/30 mixture — wipe front-to-back, never circular</li>
<li>Check paint surface temperature: 18–27 °C (65–80 °F) optimal</li>
<li>Remove hood emblem or flag around it per customer preference</li>
<li>Pre-cut film 10 cm oversize on all edges; pre-shrink leading edge if cast film</li>
<li>Knifeless tape set on rear edge of hood if wrap terminates there rather than underside wrap</li>
<li>Have slip solution in a fine-mist bottle ready; dry squeegee + felt edge tool staged</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Hood — Full Install Sequence

1. Fold film in half lengthwise with liner facing out; position over hood to verify coverage and grain direction
2. Fold back top half; peel liner from center section only; mist slip solution generously on paint and exposed adhesive
3. Lay center section onto hood using squeegee from center outward in overlapping horizontal strokes, 60° angle, light pressure
4. Work toward the front edge (grille lip); use consistent squeegee strokes to push air out at 5–10 cm intervals
5. Unfold the held-back half; peel liner while holding film taut above the panel; squeegee center-out as before
6. Squeegee toward the rear edge — if wrapping to underside, lift and re-position film at the hood lip; stretch film over the lip in 5 cm sections using thumb and hard card
7. Work the side edges: tuck along each fender line using fingertips to pre-stretch then squeegee firmly
8. At the front corners (compound curve), relief cut or small V-notch to allow film to conform without bridging
9. Post-heat the entire surface at 60–65 °C; re-squeegee while warm to activate adhesive and eliminate micro-bubbles
10. Post-heat all wrapped edges at 70 °C; pull gently outward while applying heat; squeegee edge flat against underside
11. Trim any relief cuts cleanly using a fresh blade at 20° angle; seal cut edges with edge sealer
12. Final QC pass under 3-bulb inspection light: check for bubbles, fish-eyes, lifting edges, and uneven seams

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No bubbles larger than 2 mm at 48 hours (small air pockets may self-heal under heat)</li>
<li>All edges are adhered flat with no curl or lift</li>
<li>Film grain / pattern is consistent from front to rear with no twist</li>
<li>No squeegee scratches visible under raking light</li>
<li>Front edge terminates cleanly at grille line or under lip — not visible from 1 m</li>
<li>No stretch distortion or whitening at corners</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On vehicles with heavily sculpted hood lines, pre-crease the film lightly at each character line before laying — this prevents the bridging that causes long-term lifting. Warm film at 35–40 °C before the panel lay in cold environments.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Do not post-heat with a heat gun held stationary — keep the gun moving at 15 cm/s minimum to prevent hot spots. On metallic and color-shift films, directional consistency is critical — mark the film top with tape before cutting.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Large bubble after 48 hours | Moisture or contamination trapped under film | Lift corner at nearest edge; remove, clean, re-apply | Thorough IPA wipe before install; dry panel completely |
| Edge lifting within 7 days | Insufficient post-heat or edge sealer missed | Re-heat edge to 70 °C and re-squeegee; apply edge sealer | Post-heat protocol completed on every edge |
| Stretch whitening at corner | Over-stretched film; too much tension applied | Remove and re-cut with deeper relief; use more slip during application | Pre-heat film before corner stretching |
| Fish-eye contamination | Silicone or oil on paint surface before application | Remove film; clay bar; wipe with IPA 99%; re-apply | Panel prep checklist always completed |



### Roof Panel — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Typical time:** 60–90 min · **Film orientation:** Grain runs front-to-back; match hood grain if same film run


Roof wraps offer dramatic visual impact on color-change and gloss-black applications. The main challenges are managing film in open air (UV and wind) and accessing all edges cleanly, particularly the rear edge that terminates at the windshield header.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Remove roof rack, antenna, and any trim pieces that can be safely detached</li>
<li>IPA wipe roof surface — do not contaminate neighboring panels</li>
<li>Ensure ambient temperature is stable — avoid wrapping in direct sunlight (heat causes premature adhesion)</li>
<li>Stage a helper or use a tension frame to hold the oversize sheet above the roof during positioning</li>
<li>Knifeless tape set along windshield header and rear window header if wrapping to the glass seal</li>
<li>Pre-cut film 12 cm oversize on all four sides</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Roof Panel Install Sequence

1. With a helper, carry the pre-sized film above the roof and align front-to-back; mark the center of the film with a small piece of tape
2. Hold film taut approximately 10 cm above the roof; peel liner starting from center, fold liner outward on each side as you go
3. Lay the center strip from front header to rear header in a single continuous stroke — do not allow the film to touch the panel and then lift
4. Squeegee from center outward in horizontal passes, maintaining consistent 60° angle
5. Work toward both long side edges simultaneously; tuck film under any existing trim strip or squeegee firmly down the drip rail
6. At the A-pillar corners (front), allow excess film to overlap; post-heat and fold over the pillar edge or trim at the edge seam
7. At the rear header, run knifeless tape and cut cleanly at the glass seal line
8. Post-heat the full surface at 60 °C while re-squeegee passes are made
9. Post-heat all four edges at 70 °C; seal immediately
10. Replace any removed trim pieces; check that clips are secure and film is not pinched

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Film grain aligns with hood within 5° angular tolerance</li>
<li>No bridging across the center roof bow (common on full-size trucks and SUVs)</li>
<li>All four edges are sealed and flat</li>
<li>No bubbles under raking inspection light at 24 hours</li>
<li>Drip rail film is flush — no proud edge that a fingernail catches</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On panoramic sunroof vehicles, the film terminates at the glass seal. Use knifeless tape for a clean cut line and avoid blade-cutting over the glass.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Wind is the enemy of large panel installs. Close bay doors or use a temporary curtain. A gust at the wrong moment can crease the entire sheet, wasting the material.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Center bow bridging | Film not pressed into contour; insufficient slip application | Lift section; re-heat; press with felt squeegee into contour | Use extra slip in the center area; work into contour as first move |
| Drip rail proud edge | Film folded rather than stretched over the rail | Re-heat; use a firm tuck tool to fold film under rail | Use a tucking wedge to guide film under rail during application |
| Rear header waviness | Film not pulled taught before squeegee pass | Lift and re-do the rear quarter of the panel | Always squeegee from front to rear on the roof — never side-to-side first |



### Front Bumper — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Advanced · **Typical time:** 90–150 min · **Film orientation:** N/A — film conforms to surface; no directional restriction


The front bumper is the most complex panel on the vehicle due to compound curves, deep air intakes, license plate recesses, fog light housings, lower valance transitions, and the need for a seamless appearance at the top edge where it meets the hood. This panel demands precision, patience, and willingness to re-do sections that do not conform cleanly.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Remove the bumper from the vehicle if the shop has the capability — off-car install produces superior results on complex bumpers</li>
<li>Remove fog light bezels, active grille shutters, radar modules, or any removable components</li>
<li>Clay bar and IPA wipe all surfaces including deep recesses</li>
<li>Ensure recess areas are free of old adhesive or contamination</li>
<li>Pre-measure and cut multiple pieces: top main section; lower valance; side transitions; recess inserts</li>
<li>Lay out all pieces before starting — a bumper is multi-piece by design, not a failure</li>
<li>Stage full detail light, heat gun, tucking tools, felt squeegees, and multiple blade handles</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Front Bumper Install Sequence

1. Install the top edge piece first: align the top edge of the film at the hood/bumper seam line; squeegee from center downward
2. Work the film into the upper character line of the bumper using fingertip pressure then felt squeegee
3. At the fog light housing: relief cut radiating from each corner; fold film into the recess; squeegee each facet
4. At the lower grille opening: cut the opening with a channel blade; wrap the film edge into the grille channel
5. For the lower valance section: apply as a separate piece using knifeless tape at the transition point
6. Side sections wrap around to the door jamb edge: pre-stretch film before contact using heat; fold to inside of bumper
7. All relief cuts are cleaned with a fresh blade at 20°; overlapping corners are trimmed to a clean single layer
8. Post-heat the complete assembly section by section at 65 °C
9. Post-heat all folded edges at 70 °C and squeegee firmly
10. Reinstall removed components; check that panel gap at hood is even and film seam is not visible at normal viewing distance

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No bridging across any character line or recess opening</li>
<li>Hood-to-bumper seam is clean and parallel to the body line</li>
<li>Fog light housing area shows no wrinkles or bridging</li>
<li>Lower edge is wrapped cleanly to the underside of the valance — no peeling or flutter at highway speed</li>
<li>Film color/texture matches adjacent panels (if full vehicle wrap)</li>
<li>No silver liner or adhesive exposed at any cut edge</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Cut a paper template of complex recess areas before cutting the film. A 10-minute templating session prevents a wasted piece of film. On modern bumpers with radar systems, never squeegee directly over the sensor — film bridges are acceptable and sometimes required.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Radar sensors (adaptive cruise, blind spot monitors) may be adversely affected by metallic wrap films or dark tint films. If the vehicle has these systems, use non-metallic film on bumper faces and note this on the work order.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bridging at grille opening | Film not cut and folded into channel | Carefully cut opening; fold edges into channel | Always plan opening cuts before application |
| Wrinkle at corner transition | Insufficient relief cuts or wrong cut placement | Add additional relief cuts; heat and re-form | Pre-test on a practice piece of similar geometry |
| Seam at hood gap too visible | Film terminated at wrong line | Re-cut with knifeless tape for clean line; re-evaluate panel gap | Measure twice; use knifeless tape for all visible seams |
| Film peeling from lower valance | Low adhesion area — paint often textured or rubberized | Clean thoroughly; use primer on textured areas; re-apply | Always check valance surface type before selecting film |



### Rear Bumper — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Typical time:** 60–90 min · **Film orientation:** N/A — match body color direction if applicable


The rear bumper shares many challenges with the front bumper but is typically less complex due to fewer fog lights and sensors. The main challenges are the trunk-to-bumper seam management, exhaust cutouts, and the lower kickplate area that experiences high contact wear.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Clean and IPA wipe including the lower kickplate and exhaust areas</li>
<li>Check for rock chip damage on the kickplate area — document for the customer</li>
<li>Remove exhaust tips or trim rings if removable</li>
<li>Knifeless tape set at trunk-to-bumper panel line for a clean seam</li>
<li>Pre-cut main piece with 10 cm overage on all edges</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Rear Bumper Install Sequence

1. Start at the trunk-to-bumper seam: align film using knifeless tape as guide; squeegee downward from the seam line
2. Work down the main bumper face using center-out squeegee technique
3. Exhaust cutout: let film lay over the opening; cut X-relief; fold flaps inward; trim flush to opening lip
4. Side wrap-around: pre-heat film; fold to door jamb or body panel edge
5. Lower kickplate: apply separately if texture changes; this area may require a scuff-resistant laminate overpatch on high-wear vehicles
6. Post-heat full panel at 65 °C; post-heat all edges at 70 °C; seal with edge sealer on all exposed cut edges

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Trunk-to-bumper seam line is straight and parallel to the body gap</li>
<li>Exhaust cutouts are clean with no visible adhesive at the opening</li>
<li>Lower kickplate coverage is complete and firmly adhered</li>
<li>No bridging at trunk lip transition</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Use a kickplate-specific wear-resistant overlay on fleet vehicles and work trucks — standard cast film will show scuffs within months on bumpers that see regular loading.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Exhaust heat will degrade film if the exhaust exits are pointed directly at the bumper face. Verify exhaust direction and advise the customer if their exhaust configuration is a risk to the film.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Film lifting from kickplate within 30 days | Surface contamination from boot polish or wax | Solvent clean; re-apply; educate customer on products | Ask customers about boot contact area before application |
| Exhaust cutout edge melting | Exhaust heat proximity | Re-cut with increased clearance; use heat-resistant barrier tape | Inspect exhaust routing before cutting; maintain clearance |



### Front Fender (Driver Side) — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate · **Typical time:** 45–75 min · **Film orientation:** Grain runs front-to-back; match hood direction


Fenders are a good entry-level wrap panel — predominantly flat with predictable compound curves at the wheel arch and A-pillar. The primary challenge is the wheel arch edge, which requires precise trimming for a clean visible line.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>IPA wipe including the wheel arch inner lip and the door jamb area at the front edge</li>
<li>Remove any wheel arch trim clips if the film must be tucked behind them</li>
<li>Mark the door jamb edge with knifeless tape for the rear panel termination</li>
<li>Pre-cut film 10 cm oversize on all sides</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Front Fender (Driver Side) Install Sequence

1. Position film on the panel with overage at wheel arch, A-pillar, and door gap; verify grain orientation
2. Peel liner from center; apply slip solution; lay using center-out squeegee technique
3. Work down to the wheel arch: pre-heat film to 40 °C; stretch gradually around the arch curve; use relief cuts at the tightest radius point
4. Wheel arch termination: fold film to the inside of the arch using a tuck tool; squeegee flat; trim flush at the arch lip edge
5. Door gap edge: use knifeless tape to cut a clean seam at the body line; fold remainder into the jamb
6. A-pillar edge: fold film around the pillar or terminate cleanly at the fender-to-pillar seam line
7. Post-heat full panel; post-heat wheel arch fold at 70 °C

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Wheel arch inner lip shows no visible adhesive or rough trim from outside</li>
<li>Door gap termination is consistent — same depth fold on both sides of the vehicle</li>
<li>Grain aligns with the hood within visual tolerance</li>
<li>No pull-marks or stretch patterns visible under raking light</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On vehicles where the fender and hood share an exposed edge seam, use knifeless tape on both pieces to create matching parallel seam lines — nothing makes a wrap look amateurish faster than mismatched seam placement.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Fender vents and louvers require individual film sections per louver blade — never stretch a single piece over a vent grille. The result will bridge and lift within weeks.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wheel arch lifting in winter | Insufficient post-heat at arch fold; ice and water intrusion | Re-heat arch; add edge sealer; advise customer on winter wash care | Use edge sealer on all wheel arch fold edges at install |
| Grain mismatch with hood | Film sheet laid at wrong angle | Re-do from scratch after confirming grain orientation | Mark grain direction with tape on all pieces before cutting |



### Door Panel (Full) — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate · **Typical time:** 45–60 min · **Film orientation:** Grain runs front-to-back


Full door wraps are a staple panel that installers at all experience levels must master. The main challenge is the door handle recess and the character lines that cross the panel, which require careful squeegee angle management to prevent bridging.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Remove door handle if possible (remove-and-reinstall method produces cleaner results than wrapping around)</li>
<li>IPA wipe entire door face and edges</li>
<li>Open the door to provide edge access; place a soft pad on the rocker to protect it from the door edge during wrapping</li>
<li>Knifeless tape at door jamb edges for clean termination on all four sides</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Door Panel (Full) Install Sequence

1. Verify film sizing: should cover door face with 5 cm overlap into door jamb on all four edges
2. Position and align film; peel liner; apply slip solution; lay from center outward
3. At the character line (horizontal crease across panel): slow down and change squeegee angle to 30° to feed film into the line rather than bridging over it
4. Door handle recess: if handle removed — film the recess fully, then install handle over film for the cleanest result. If not removed — relief cut around recess; fold film edges into the pocket
5. Work all four door edges into the jamb: top edge under the seal; side edges into the gap; bottom edge under the rocker cap
6. Post-heat full panel at 60 °C; post-heat all jamb folds at 70 °C
7. Re-install door handle; verify handle function and alignment

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No bridging over character lines — run a fingernail along the line; film should follow the contour</li>
<li>Door handle area shows no wrinkles or lifting when handle is at rest and when pulled</li>
<li>All four jamb edges are tucked a minimum of 20 mm into the jamb</li>
<li>Bottom edge is protected against car wash brush abrasion</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

Character lines with a sharp V-profile (greater than 45° included angle) may require a horizontal relief slit at the deepest point of the line to allow the film to conform without bridging.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Never apply heat to the door glass edge with a heat gun — the sudden thermal shock can crack the glass in cold weather. Use a hot damp towel to warm the glass seal area instead.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bridging at character line | Squeegee angle too steep; film not worked into the crease | Re-heat; use a stiff felt edge tool to press film into line | Work slowly at character lines; lower squeegee angle |
| Handle area lifting | Insufficient adhesion at curved handle pocket | Remove handle; clean pocket; re-apply; seal edges | Always prefer remove-and-reinstall method for door handles |



### Mirror Cap (Each) — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Typical time:** 30–45 min per mirror · **Film orientation:** N/A — conform-to-shape application


Mirror caps offer a high visual-impact accent opportunity (gloss black, carbon fiber, color-change) and are frequently done as standalone upgrades. Despite their small size, they require precise technique due to the compound curves and the need to wrap to the inside mounting surface.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Remove mirror from vehicle if possible — off-car is strongly preferred for mirror caps</li>
<li>Remove any chrome or trim inserts from the mirror shell</li>
<li>IPA wipe all surfaces including the inner mounting face</li>
<li>Assess the cap geometry: most mirrors require at least two pieces (front face + rear face) or one piece with multiple relief cuts</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Mirror Cap (Each) Install Sequence

1. Start with the front face: lay film over the mirror cap with generous overage (5 cm minimum on all sides)
2. From the flat top surface, squeegee outward toward the edges
3. As the mirror face curves downward at the leading edge, pre-heat film to 40 °C; gradually stretch using fingertips, working in 2 cm increments
4. At the bottom curve (most compound area on most mirrors): alternate heat and stretch in 1–2 cm increments; use a felt squeegee to press each section before moving to the next
5. At the trailing edge (where mirror meets the door), fold excess film to the inside mounting surface
6. Trim excess neatly at the inner edge; seal all cut edges
7. Repeat for the mirror bottom face if applicable; match film grain/pattern with front face
8. Post-heat the entire assembly at 65 °C; re-squeegee any areas that have not fully adhered
9. Re-install on vehicle; check that film is not pinched at the mounting points

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>No bubbles visible from any normal viewing angle</li>
<li>Film wraps cleanly to the inside of the cap with no visible white adhesive from behind</li>
<li>Both mirrors are matched — no visible difference in texture orientation or sheen</li>
<li>No wrinkles at the leading edge curve — this is the most common failure point</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On small mirror caps, work with minimal slip solution — too much slip makes it difficult to control positioning on the small surface. A dry or near-dry application with frequent heat assists is often cleaner.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Camera-equipped mirrors (blind spot cameras, 360° view cameras) have glass camera lenses that must not be scratched by squeegees or contaminated with adhesive. Mask camera lenses before starting the wrap.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wrinkle at leading edge curve | Insufficient heat; film not pre-stretched | Re-heat; lift; stretch incrementally | Use more heat before each section at the leading curve |
| Film not adhering to inner mounting face | Mounting surface often textured ABS — low adhesion | Scuff surface lightly; use application primer | Primer all ABS mounting surfaces before wrapping |



### A-Pillar (Left & Right) — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate · **Typical time:** 20–35 min per pillar · **Film orientation:** Film runs vertically top-to-bottom


A-pillars are visible transition pieces between the hood/fender and the windshield. Most are wrapped with a single narrow piece that folds slightly to the windshield glass seal edge. Accuracy is critical as the seam is at eye level from outside the vehicle.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>IPA wipe entire pillar including the glass seal channel</li>
<li>Remove any existing vinyl or film; if previous film, clean adhesive completely</li>
<li>If the A-pillar has a molded end cap, remove and wrap the cap separately for a cleaner result</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### A-Pillar (Left & Right) Install Sequence

1. Pre-cut a strip slightly wider than the pillar plus 15 mm on the glass side and 10 mm on the door-gap side
2. Position and align the strip vertically — the top end tucks under the roof film or trim
3. Squeegee from the center of the pillar face outward toward the glass edge
4. Fold the glass-side edge into the rubber seal channel using a tuck wedge tool
5. The outer door-gap edge folds into the fender-to-door gap or terminates at the body seam line
6. Post-heat the full pillar at 65 °C; re-squeegee; seal glass-side edge with clear edge sealer

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Pillar coverage is gap-free from the top trim to the lower corner of the windshield glass</li>
<li>Glass seal edge is fully tucked — no visible adhesive or film edge from inside or outside the vehicle</li>
<li>The pillar seam at the door gap is aligned with the door panel seam (if door panel is also being wrapped)</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On narrow pillars, keep film slightly undersized at the glass edge — it is easier to pull the seal back and tuck than to have excess film fold over into the glass.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Avoid pulling aggressively on the windshield seal to tuck film — seals can deform or tear, creating a water leak. Work slowly with a flexible tuck wedge.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Film lifting at glass seal edge | Seal moisture; insufficient tuck depth | Re-heat; re-tuck; apply edge sealer | Ensure glass seal area is thoroughly dry; use a dry-brush before tucking |
| Film too short at top | Incorrect measurement before cutting | Re-cut with correct measurement; overlap under roof trim | Always measure with trim removed; add 15 mm extra at top |



### Roof Pillars (B & C) — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Typical time:** 30–45 min per pillar set · **Film orientation:** Vertical grain; match A-pillar orientation


B and C pillars are wrapped similarly to A pillars but may be wider and have additional complexity from door and window seals on both sides. They are often wrapped in gloss black or carbon fiber for accent purposes.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>IPA wipe all pillar surfaces; ensure door seals are clean and free of protectants</li>
<li>Remove any trim plates or badges on the pillar</li>
<li>Determine whether film wraps to the inside of the door opening or terminates at the visible face edge</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Roof Pillars (B & C) Install Sequence

1. Cut a piece sized for the full pillar face with 20 mm overage on the front and rear glass edges
2. Align the piece vertically; squeegee from center outward
3. Fold the front glass seal edge into the rubber channel
4. Fold the rear glass seal edge into its channel
5. Top and bottom edges tuck under body trim or fold into the door opening
6. Post-heat at 65 °C; seal all tucked edges with edge sealer

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Both sides of the vehicle are symmetrical — same exposure, same sheen, same seam placement</li>
<li>No bridging between glass seal channels</li>
<li>Door seals re-compress and seal correctly after reinstallation — test with a water hose if concerned</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

If both glass seals on the B-pillar are present (common on 4-door sedans), the piece is effectively a band between two channels. Pre-stretch the film slightly in the horizontal direction before laying to prevent the film from pulling out of one channel when the other is tucked.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

C-pillar wraps on SUVs often cover the third-row window seal. Check that the door-actuated glass still seals correctly after the wrap. An improperly tucked edge can deform the seal and cause water intrusion.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Film pulling out of one seal while tucking the other | Insufficient film width; film under tension | Re-cut wider piece; tuck both sides simultaneously with two tuck tools | Always pre-stretch before laying between two tight channels |



### Trunk Lid / Decklid — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Typical time:** 60–90 min · **Film orientation:** Grain runs left-to-right on most trunk lids; verify against body line


The trunk lid is similar to the hood in technique but is typically smaller and often has a prominent lip or spoiler that requires a separate piece or a complex fold. On sedans, the trunk lip is one of the most visible edges on the vehicle.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Remove trunk-mounted spoilers or spoiler brackets when possible for cleaner edge wrapping</li>
<li>Remove license plate and license plate lights</li>
<li>IPA wipe including the trunk lip underside — this is often a high-adhesion challenge area</li>
<li>Assess spoiler mounting: does the film pass under the spoiler gasket or terminate at the spoiler edge?</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Trunk Lid / Decklid Install Sequence

1. Start film at the front edge of the trunk lid (nearest to the cabin)
2. Squeegee from center outward and rearward
3. At the character line above the trunk lip, use a low squeegee angle to work film into the feature line
4. At the rear lip edge: pre-heat film; stretch over the lip; fold down the inside of the lip at least 20 mm
5. If a spoiler is present: run the film under the spoiler gasket or terminate with knifeless tape at the spoiler base — never bridge under the spoiler gap as the lift will cause failure
6. Replace license plate surround and lights after trimming film around lamp housing
7. Post-heat full lid at 60 °C; post-heat lip fold at 70 °C; seal all cut edges

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Trunk lip fold is a minimum 20 mm onto the underside — deeper is better</li>
<li>License plate area is clean with no adhesive visible in the lamp housing</li>
<li>If spoiler is present, the seam at the spoiler base is flush and not visible from rear view</li>
<li>Grain direction matches rear body panels for coherent appearance</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On vehicles with an integrated lip spoiler (part of the trunk lid metal), the fold-over on the rear lip is the most visible edge on the vehicle from behind. Take extra time here — it is what people see at a red light.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Trunk wiring harnesses for the license plate lights often run along the trunk lip. Verify the harness is positioned inside the fold before squeegeeing the film flat — pinching the harness can cause electrical shorts.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Film bridging at character line above lip | Squeegee angle too high; insufficient feed into line | Re-heat; use felt edge tool to press into line | Slow down; change angle; test on a cold press first |
| Trunk lip fold peeling in winter | Cold-temperature adhesion failure; insufficient depth | Warm to 70 °C; re-fold; extend fold depth; edge sealer | Post-heat lip to 70 °C at install; minimum 20 mm fold |



### Rocker Panels — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Beginner · **Typical time:** 30–45 min (both sides) · **Film orientation:** Grain runs front-to-back


Rocker panels are low-mounted, predominantly flat panels that run between the front and rear wheel arches. They are a high-impact zone for road debris, curb rash, and door dings from tight parking. Wrapping them in a durable cast film significantly extends their cosmetic life.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Clean thoroughly — rockers accumulate tar, road grime, and salt; use a dedicated fallout remover before IPA wipe</li>
<li>Inspect for any rust bubbling — document and inform the customer; film will trap moisture if installed over active rust</li>
<li>Check wheel arch-to-rocker transition — is the film a single piece or does it terminate at the arch lip?</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Rocker Panels Install Sequence

1. Measure the rocker length and height; cut a single piece for each side with 10 cm overage top and bottom
2. Lay from the center of the rocker outward — this is a relatively simple flat application
3. Top edge: tuck under the door sill plate or fold to the inside of the door opening
4. Bottom edge: fold under the rocker lip to the underside; secure with squeegee
5. Front and rear ends: terminate at the wheel arch with a clean cut or fold into the arch
6. Post-heat full panel at 60 °C; post-heat all folds at 70 °C

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Top edge is invisible from outside — either under sill plate or tucked into door opening</li>
<li>Bottom fold is a minimum 15 mm — withstands high-speed air at 100 km/h without lifting</li>
<li>No bubbles that will accumulate moisture and cause rust acceleration</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On lowered vehicles, the rocker panel may be extremely close to the ground. Assess whether the wrap will be scraped on speed bumps and advise the customer accordingly.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Always inspect for active rust or rust bubbling before wrapping rockers. Film wrapped over active rust accelerates rust spread by trapping moisture — this is a liability and quality issue that must be disclosed.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bottom edge flutter at highway speed | Insufficient fold depth; fold not post-heated | Re-heat; increase fold depth to minimum 20 mm; add edge sealer | Minimum 20 mm fold on all rocker bottom edges; always edge seal |
| Film contaminated with tar at application | Rocker not fully decontaminated before install | Remove film; solvent decontaminate; re-apply | Use tar remover before IPA wipe on all rocker panels |



### Full Rear Quarter Panel — Master Procedure


**Difficulty:** Advanced · **Typical time:** 90–120 min · **Film orientation:** Grain runs front-to-back; match door panels


The rear quarter panel is often the largest single body panel on a vehicle and presents the most complex curves, particularly on sedans and coupes where the wheel arch blends into the rear door area. Managing the wheel arch, the C-pillar transition, and the trunk/quarter edge seam are the three primary challenges.



#### Pre-Install Checklist


<ul class="checklist">
<li>IPA wipe entire quarter panel including the inner wheel arch lip</li>
<li>Determine panel seam strategy: will the quarter film overlap or butt-join the door film?</li>
<li>Knifeless tape set at the trunk/quarter edge for clean termination</li>
<li>Remove any side emblems or badging — this is mandatory on large quarter panel installations</li>
<li>Pre-cut film with 15 cm overage on all four edges — err toward more on the wheel arch side</li>
</ul>



<div class="procedure">

### Full Rear Quarter Panel Install Sequence

1. Position and align the film for grain direction; center the panel on the film sheet
2. Peel liner and apply slip solution to the full panel
3. Lay film starting from the vertical center of the panel; squeegee horizontally outward
4. Work toward the wheel arch: slowest, most deliberate section of this panel; pre-heat film to 40 °C; work in 3 cm increments around the arch
5. At the tightest radius of the wheel arch, multiple small relief cuts allow the film to conform; each cut is immediately squeegeed down
6. Inner wheel arch fold: a minimum 20 mm fold to the inside of the arch with edge sealer
7. C-pillar transition: film terminates at the C-pillar with a clean seam or overlaps by 5 mm under the pillar trim
8. Rear edge at trunk panel: use knifeless tape for a clean, straight seam at the quarter-to-trunk seam line
9. Post-heat full panel section by section at 60–65 °C; post-heat wheel arch fold at 70 °C

</div>



#### Quality Checkpoints


<ul class="checklist">
<li>Wheel arch fold minimum 20 mm; no visible adhesive from below the car</li>
<li>Grain is continuous and uninterrupted from front of quarter to rear (no twist)</li>
<li>C-pillar seam or transition is clean and aligns with adjacent panel seams</li>
<li>Rear quarter-to-trunk seam is straight, parallel to the body gap, and consistent in width</li>
<li>No bridging over any character line or raised body feature</li>
</ul>



<div class="callout tip">

On heavily styled rear quarters (e.g., wide-body conversions or fastback coupes), break the panel into two pieces split at the character line. Each piece conforms much more cleanly, and a knifeless tape seam at the character line is invisible from any normal viewing angle.

</div>


<div class="callout warning">

Parking sensor modules embedded in the rear quarter panel on some vehicles can be dislodged during aggressive squeegeeing. Work with reduced pressure in known sensor locations and test sensor function before delivery.

</div>



#### Common Defects on This Panel


### Troubleshooting Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Corrective Action | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wheel arch lifting at 3-month mark | Edge sealer not applied; winter water infiltration | Re-heat; re-fold with deeper fold; seal thoroughly | Edge sealer is mandatory on all wheel arch folds |
| Twist in grain across long panel | Film sheet not perfectly aligned at start | Remove entire panel film; re-lay with alignment guides | Mark grain direction on film back before positioning |
