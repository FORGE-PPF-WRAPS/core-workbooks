Chapter 2 — TPU Film Science & Product Selection

What Is Paint Protection Film?

Modern PPF is a multi-layer thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU) laminate engineered to absorb impact energy, resist staining, and self-heal light surface marring.

Parameter Specification Notes
Overall thickness 6–8 mil typical Varies by product tier
Top coat 0.5 mil elastomeric Self-healing, stain resistance
TPU body 5–6 mil Impact absorption
Adhesive 1 mil pressure-sensitive Removable without paint damage
Release liner Polyester Removed at install

Film Categories

Category Appearance Primary Use
Standard gloss clear Invisible on factory paint Partial front, full front
Matte/satin clear Reduces gloss uniformly Stealth packages
Colored TPU Pigmented protection Color-change with protection
Textured Carbon/brushed patterns Accent panels

Self-Healing Behavior

The elastomeric top coat flows back when heated above approximately 140°F. Deep gouges through the TPU body cannot self-heal — set customer expectations accurately.

Never promise that PPF prevents all damage. PPF dramatically reduces everyday wear; it does not make paint invincible against major impacts.

Customer Profile Recommended Tier Package Starting Point
Daily commuter highway miles Premium gloss TPU Partial front + rockers
Luxury/exotic Top-tier optical clarity Full front minimum
Off-road / gravel Thicker impact-rated TPU Full front + rockers
Lease return protection Standard gloss High-impact zones only
Matte factory paint Matte PPF matched to sheen Full front

Hood Panel — Master Procedure

Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Intermediate · Typical time: 90–150 min · Film orientation: Pattern aligned to factory body lines

The hood is the highest-visibility PPF panel and the foundation of every partial-front package. Success depends on clean anchor placement, controlled slip, and post-heat along crown lines.

Pre-Install Checklist

  • Document pre-existing chips and scratches with photos
  • Complete full wash, clay, iron removal, and IPA wipe
  • Confirm paint correction is complete before pattern placement
  • Stage hood in shade; surface temp below 95°F
  • Lay pattern on clean surface; verify orientation and overlap marks
  • Mix fresh slip solution; stage heat gun and multiple squeegee durometers
  • Mask wiper cowl and leading edge if customer requests
  • Verify plotter pattern includes mirror of OEM badge cutouts if applicable

Hood Panel Install Sequence

  1. Position pattern on hood; tape hinge side as anchor reference
  2. Peel 6-inch release strip along top edge; mist adhesive with slip
  3. Lay top edge; align centerline to hood crease
  4. Squeegee anchor band across top with firm overlapping passes
  5. Peel liner in 12-inch sections while partner mists adhesive
  6. Work from center outward using wet squeegee technique
  7. Relieve tension at crown with light heat and micro-adjustments
  8. Address light fingers at corners with localized heat and stretch
  9. Wrap leading edge under lip using soft squeegee and heat assist
  10. Tuck film into seam at fender junction without bridging
  11. Trim excess at perimeter with fresh blade and light pressure
  12. Post-heat entire panel 200–220°F moving continuously
  13. Inspect optically for silvering, contamination, and edge lift

Quality Checkpoints

  • No trapped contamination under film
  • Edges wrapped or tucked per shop standard
  • No silvering or adhesive starvation visible
  • Leading edge sealed with no water channels
  • Post-heat completed on all stretched zones
  • Customer pre-damage documented separately

Anchor the flat crown first. Stretch only where geometry demands it.

Never bridge a body seam with tension. Fresh paint under 30 days must not be wrapped.

Front Bumper Cover — Master Procedure

Difficulty: ★★★★☆ Advanced · Typical time: 2–4 hours · Film orientation: Multiple pieces; bumper off-vehicle preferred

Modern bumper covers combine deep recesses, sensor pockets, and compound curves. Bumper-off installation dramatically improves edge quality and reduces rework.

Pre-Install Checklist

  • Remove bumper per OEM service procedure when possible
  • Clean backside channels and fog light pockets
  • Identify ADAS sensor zones; use sensor-safe film if required
  • Template or plot all pieces before adhesive contact
  • Stage heat gun, relief cut tools, and soft squeegees
  • Verify paint is fully cured on repainted bumpers
  • Photograph parking sensors and trim positions
  • Lay pieces in install order: center lower, upper, sides

Front Bumper Cover Install Sequence

  1. Install center lower piece first as primary anchor
  2. Work upward in overlapping sections per pattern guide
  3. Heat and relief-cut only where pattern indicates
  4. Set fog light and grille surround pieces before outer corners
  5. Use foam squeegee in deep recesses; hard card on flat zones
  6. Stretch into pockets with heat; never cold-stretch TPU
  7. Tuck all perimeter edges into factory seams
  8. Trim at invisible lines behind bumper lip
  9. Reinstall bumper on vehicle for final edge check
  10. Align film seams to factory panel gaps
  11. Post-heat all compound curves and recessed areas
  12. Final QC with cross-light at multiple angles

Quality Checkpoints

  • No lifted edges at grille surround
  • Sensor areas clear and film not bridging vents
  • Seams aligned to factory gaps
  • No contamination in recesses
  • Post-heat completed on all stretched areas

Bumper-off saves hours on complex covers. Label every piece before removal.

Do not cover active sensor faces unless using approved sensor film.

Hood Panel — Master Procedure

Difficulty: ★★★☆☆ Intermediate · Typical time: 90–150 min · Film orientation: Pattern aligned to factory body lines

The hood is the highest-visibility PPF panel and the foundation of every partial-front package. Success depends on clean anchor placement, controlled slip, and post-heat along crown lines.

Pre-Install Checklist

  • Document pre-existing chips and scratches with photos
  • Complete full wash, clay, iron removal, and IPA wipe
  • Confirm paint correction is complete before pattern placement
  • Stage hood in shade; surface temp below 95°F
  • Lay pattern on clean surface; verify orientation and overlap marks
  • Mix fresh slip solution; stage heat gun and multiple squeegee durometers
  • Mask wiper cowl and leading edge if customer requests
  • Verify plotter pattern includes mirror of OEM badge cutouts if applicable

Hood Panel Install Sequence

  1. Position pattern on hood; tape hinge side as anchor reference
  2. Peel 6-inch release strip along top edge; mist adhesive with slip
  3. Lay top edge; align centerline to hood crease
  4. Squeegee anchor band across top with firm overlapping passes
  5. Peel liner in 12-inch sections while partner mists adhesive
  6. Work from center outward using wet squeegee technique
  7. Relieve tension at crown with light heat and micro-adjustments
  8. Address light fingers at corners with localized heat and stretch
  9. Wrap leading edge under lip using soft squeegee and heat assist
  10. Tuck film into seam at fender junction without bridging
  11. Trim excess at perimeter with fresh blade and light pressure
  12. Post-heat entire panel 200–220°F moving continuously
  13. Inspect optically for silvering, contamination, and edge lift

Quality Checkpoints

  • No trapped contamination under film
  • Edges wrapped or tucked per shop standard
  • No silvering or adhesive starvation visible
  • Leading edge sealed with no water channels
  • Post-heat completed on all stretched zones
  • Customer pre-damage documented separately

Anchor the flat crown first. Stretch only where geometry demands it.

Never bridge a body seam with tension. Fresh paint under 30 days must not be wrapped.
