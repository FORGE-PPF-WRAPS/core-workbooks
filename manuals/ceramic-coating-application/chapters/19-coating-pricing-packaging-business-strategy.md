# Chapter 19 — Coating Pricing, Packaging & Business Strategy

The ceramic coating service category offers among the highest gross margin per hour of any automotive restyling service when priced and sold correctly. This chapter provides the complete pricing framework, bundling strategy, and business model considerations for establishing a profitable coating operation within any shop configuration.

## Fully-Loaded Cost Analysis for Coating Services

Many operators price coating services based only on material cost and a rough time estimate. This consistently leads to underpricing because the true cost of delivering a professional coating includes several categories that are frequently overlooked.

| Cost Category | Typical Amount | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Coating product cost per vehicle | $25–$150 depending on tier and vehicle size | Calculate in cost per ml; measure product used per vehicle for 3 jobs; average for your per-vehicle cost |
| Applicator and leveling cloth cost | $8–$20 per vehicle | Suede blocks at $3–5 each; leveling cloths at $2–4 each; 3–6 cloths per vehicle |
| IPA and prep chemical cost | $3–$8 per vehicle | IPA; distilled water; clay; iron remover portion |
| Labor (coating application only) | $80–$200 per hour × coating hours | Exclude correction time from coating labor — price that separately |
| Bay overhead (coating-specific portion) | $30–$80 per hour in use | Rent; utilities; equipment depreciation allocated to coating bay time |
| Quality control time | $30–$60 per vehicle | 30–60 minutes of QC inspection at technician rate |
| Warranty administration overhead | $15–$25 per vehicle | Warranty certificate preparation; CRM documentation; annual follow-up cost amortized |
| Marketing and sales cost (per closed job) | $50–$150 per job | Calculated from total marketing spend divided by jobs generated |

## Package Bundling Strategy

Bundles increase average job value and simplify the customer's decision. When the customer sees a pre-designed package, the decision shifts from "should I add each item?" to "which level is right for me?" — a much easier decision that consistently results in higher average transaction value.

<div class="procedure">

### Designing Effective Service Bundles

1. Always include at least three bundle tiers — the middle tier is selected most frequently when presented between a high and low anchor
2. Name bundles by outcome rather than by technical specification: "Complete Protection" vs. "3-Layer SiO₂ with 9H Hardness"
3. Every bundle includes a clear warranty summary in the marketing material — warranty differentiation is a primary value driver between tiers
4. Seasonal bundles (spring prep; winter protection) create urgency that standard ongoing packages cannot create
5. Fleet bundles should be priced per unit with a volume discount at 5+ and 10+ units; negotiate annual commitment for best pricing to the fleet operator
6. Dealer bundles are priced for F&I markup: the dealer charges the customer 1.5–2× your wholesale price; your wholesale price must still be profitable at that level
