# Chapter 35 — Complete Coating Prep Workflow — Step by Step

This chapter consolidates the complete vehicle workflow from customer drop-off through coating delivery into a single reference document. It is the operational standard operating procedure for coating technicians. Every step is sequenced precisely; no step may be skipped; each step has a defined completion criterion before the next begins.

## Phase 1 — Vehicle Intake (15–20 minutes)

<div class="procedure">

### Vehicle Intake Sequence

1. Customer and technician review the vehicle together under adequate lighting: all four sides, roof, glass, wheels, and interior — note all pre-existing damage on the intake form
2. Measure paint film thickness on all primary panels using a calibrated digital gauge: record the value for each panel in the job card
3. Photograph all panels, all glass, all wheels, and all pre-existing damage: timestamp all photos and upload to the CRM
4. Discuss service scope with customer: confirm which panels are included; confirm any panels excluded from correction or coating
5. Customer signs the service authorization form: scope confirmed; pre-existing damage acknowledged; pricing approved
6. Move vehicle into the service bay: wash bay first if arriving dirty; coating bay only after full decontamination

</div>

## Phase 2 — Wash and Decontamination (45–90 minutes)

<div class="procedure">

### Full Decontamination Sequence

1. Pre-rinse entire vehicle with water to remove loose dirt
2. Foam cannon application of pH-neutral shampoo: dwell 3 minutes; rinse from top to bottom
3. Two-bucket hand wash with wash mitt: work from roof to lower panels; rinse mitt in rinse bucket between each panel
4. Iron fallout remover applied to all paint and wheel surfaces: dwell 4–7 minutes; agitate with a soft brush on heavy fallout areas; rinse thoroughly
5. Tar and adhesive remover on lower panels, behind wheels, and any visible tar deposits: wipe with clean microfibers; rinse
6. Clay bar treatment on all paint surfaces using clay lubricant: glide in straight strokes; fold clay to expose a fresh face when it loads with contamination
7. Final rinse: remove all product residue; inspect panels while wet for any remaining contamination
8. Dry with forced-air blower and clean microfiber drying towels: ensure all panel gaps and mirrors are fully dry
9. Move vehicle to correction bay: do not introduce the vehicle to the correction area while wet

</div>

## Phase 3 — Paint Correction (2–12 hours depending on tier)

<div class="procedure">

### Paint Correction Workflow

1. Inspect all panels under 3-bulb LED inspection bar: document defect map for each panel (type and severity of defects)
2. Select correction products: choose compound grit and pad combination based on defect severity and paint hardness
3. Perform test spot on each panel: 5 cm × 5 cm; assess result under inspection light; confirm products correct the defects before committing to the full panel
4. Correct each panel systematically: use DA or rotary at the confirmed settings; 3–4 overlapping passes; maintain consistent speed and pressure
5. After correcting each panel, check paint film thickness with gauge: if any reading approaches 80 µm total or 20 µm clear coat, stop correction on that panel; document and notify customer
6. Final inspection of all corrected panels under the inspection bar: confirm defects are at or below the agreed correction target
7. IPA wipe all corrected surfaces: use 50/50 IPA and purified water; single-direction wipes; this removes any polishing oil residue that would prevent coating adhesion
8. Panel-by-panel sign-off by lead technician before coating begins: no exceptions

</div>

## Phase 4 — Coating Application (2–6 hours)

<div class="procedure">

### Coating Application Workflow

1. Transfer vehicle to the coating bay: ensure the bay is at 18–25 °C and relative humidity below 60% before proceeding
2. Confirm surface temperature with an infrared thermometer: panels must be between 15–27 °C; allow to acclimatize if outside range
3. Final IPA wipe immediately before coating: this is the last contamination removal; apply, wipe; do not let IPA dry on the surface before coating
4. Apply coating to the first panel using the applicator block method: 4–6 drops on suede applicator; apply in overlapping horizontal then vertical strokes
5. Flash time: observe the panel — typically 60–90 seconds at room temperature; the surface shifts from wet to a faint haze indicating the initial cure has begun
6. Level with a clean, flat short-nap microfiber: light pressure; straight strokes; remove all coating uniformly; no streaks or high spots allowed
7. Move to the next panel immediately; do not allow leveled panels to sit unattended — inspect every 5 minutes for any high spots developing
8. High spot management: if a high spot is discovered after leveling, apply high-spot remover on a clean cloth and buff immediately — do not dry-buff a hardening high spot
9. After all panels are coated and leveled, perform first inspection pass under the inspection bar: check every panel for uniformity and any missed areas
10. Apply second coat (where package includes) after the first coat has cured to a tack-free state: typically 1–4 hours depending on product
11. IR lamp curing: apply infrared lamp at recommended distance (typically 40–60 cm) for 20–40 minutes per panel to accelerate cross-linking and achieve handling hardness faster

</div>

## Phase 5 — Final Inspection and Delivery (30–45 minutes)

<div class="procedure">

### Delivery Preparation

1. Full vehicle inspection under the inspection bar: all panels, glass, trim, wheels — document any observations
2. Apply glass coating if included: follow glass coating protocol; allow minimum 30 minutes before delivery
3. Apply wheel coating if included: barrel, face, lug nut recesses; allow to cure before driving
4. Final exterior wipe-down: remove any fingerprints or handling marks from the coating session
5. Interior preparation: ensure interior is free of any coating overspray; clean glass interior; vacuum any debris from the detail session
6. Photograph the completed vehicle from all angles in the same positions as intake photos: final documentation
7. Customer handover: delivery walkthrough, aftercare education, care card, warranty documentation, annual inspection booking

</div>
