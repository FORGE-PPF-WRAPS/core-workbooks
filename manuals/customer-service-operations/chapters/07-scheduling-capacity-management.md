# Chapter 7 — Scheduling & Capacity Management

Scheduling is the operational backbone of a well-run shop. Poor scheduling creates technician idle time, bottlenecks, missed deadlines, and dissatisfied customers. Excellent scheduling maximizes throughput while protecting quality and setting achievable customer expectations.

## Scheduling Principles

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Never schedule a job without confirming installer capacity with the lead installer first</li>
<li>Build buffer time between jobs — a 4-hour job is scheduled in a 5-hour block</li>
<li>Complex or first-of-type vehicles require a pre-job consultation with the installer before committing to a date</li>
<li>Material lead time (custom orders, fleet film) is confirmed with the supplier before scheduling</li>
<li>Customer-requested rush timelines are cleared with the lead installer before promising them</li>
<li>No two full-vehicle wraps are scheduled in the same production slot without sufficient staff</li>
<li>Seasonal demand surges (spring, pre-summer) are managed with a waitlist and priority booking for repeat clients</li>
</ul>

## Scheduling Workflow

<div class="procedure">

### Booking a Job in the Calendar

1. Confirm estimate is approved and signed by customer
2. Confirm deposit is collected and recorded
3. Check installer availability with lead installer for the requested window
4. Confirm material availability — is the specified film/product in stock or does it need to be ordered?
5. Add the job to the production calendar with: customer name, vehicle, service, contact number, and job value
6. Send the customer a booking confirmation with: drop-off date/time, address, what to bring, loaner/transport information if applicable
7. Add the job to the CRM as "Approved / Scheduled" with all details
8. Set internal reminders: material prep 3 days before; confirmation call 24 hours before

</div>

## Rescheduling & Cancellation Handling

<div class="procedure">

### Customer-Initiated Reschedule

1. Thank the customer for notifying us — do not make them feel guilty
2. Find a new time immediately if possible: "Let me check what we have open for you"
3. Update the production calendar and CRM immediately
4. Notify the installer if the original slot is now available for another job
5. Send confirmation of the new appointment within the hour

</div>

<div class="procedure">

### Shop-Initiated Rescheduling

1. Call the customer personally — do not send a text-only notification for a schedule change we are causing
2. Explain honestly: material delay, technician availability, prior job running long
3. Offer at least two alternative dates
4. Compensate appropriately: priority rebooking, courtesy upgrade, or modest discount at shop discretion
5. Log the reason and resolution in CRM; flag for CSM review if customer expresses dissatisfaction

</div>
