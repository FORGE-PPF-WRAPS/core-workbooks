# Chapter 8 — Vehicle Intake & Walkaround Inspection

The vehicle intake walkaround is one of the most important operational moments in the customer relationship. Done properly, it protects the shop from liability for pre-existing damage, gives the customer confidence their vehicle is in good hands, and sets the foundation for an honest relationship. Skipping or rushing this step is one of the most common sources of customer disputes in automotive service.

## Why the Intake Walkaround Matters

Customers sometimes do not notice — or choose not to disclose — pre-existing damage. Without a documented walkaround, any scratch, dent, or chip discovered at or after delivery may be attributed to the shop regardless of responsibility. Conversely, a thorough walkaround demonstrates professionalism and care that competitors who skip it cannot match.

<div class="callout warning">

Never allow a vehicle into the bay without a completed and signed intake form. No exceptions. If a customer is in a rush, perform an abbreviated walk with photos and explain you are doing this to protect their vehicle — not to delay them.

</div>

## Intake Walkaround Protocol

<div class="procedure">

### Complete Vehicle Intake Walkaround

1. Ensure adequate lighting — move the vehicle to a lit area or use a detail light
2. Begin at the front bumper; move clockwise around the vehicle
3. Document every chip, scratch, dent, scuff, stain, or pre-existing film issue on the intake diagram
4. Photograph every noted defect from two angles — close and at distance for context
5. Note interior condition: stains, wear, any items of value left in the vehicle
6. Note tire condition and wheel finish condition
7. Note any pre-existing mechanical issues: door that doesn't close fully, trim that is loose, sensor that is exposed
8. Read current mileage and record on the form
9. Have the customer sign the completed intake form before taking the key
10. Give the customer a copy of the intake form; retain the original in the job file
11. Upload all intake photos to the CRM job record within one hour

</div>

## Intake Form — Required Fields

| Section | Fields |
| --- | --- |
| Customer Info | Name, phone, email, signature |
| Vehicle Info | Year, Make, Model, VIN (last 6), Color, Odometer |
| Condition Diagram | Vehicle outline (top/side/front/rear view) with defect marks |
| Defect Notes | Freeform description with photo reference numbers |
| Service Agreed | Description of service to be performed (ties to estimate) |
| Customer Acknowledgments | Pre-existing items noted; any known issues the shop has noted |
| Personal Items | Log of any items left in vehicle; disclaimer for items not logged |
| Key Receipt | How many keys provided; key type (smart key, fob, physical) |
| Date & Time | Intake date and time recorded |

## Special Intake Situations

### Vehicle with Significant Pre-Existing Paint Issues

If paint correction, active rust, deep scratches, or prior amateur wrap/film removal damage is present, the intake requires an additional step: the SA must flag the issue to the lead installer before the vehicle enters the bay.

<div class="procedure">

### Paint Issue Intake

1. Document all areas of concern on the intake form with detailed photos
2. Notify the lead installer verbally and show photos
3. Brief the customer: "We've noted some areas that may affect the final result — let me walk you through what we see"
4. Amend the estimate if additional prep work is required (correction, scratch filling, etc.)
5. Obtain the customer's written acknowledgment of the condition and their acceptance of the limitation

</div>

### Vehicles with High-Value Modifications

Vehicles with aftermarket wheels, custom paint, wraps, accessories, or track modifications require additional care documentation.

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Photograph all visible modifications at intake</li>
<li>Note any modifications that are near the service area and could be at risk</li>
<li>Discuss any modification that could interfere with the service (e.g., aero kit restricting bumper access)</li>
<li>Get explicit customer acknowledgment that the shop has been informed of all modifications</li>
<li>Assign the most experienced installer to this vehicle</li>
</ul>
