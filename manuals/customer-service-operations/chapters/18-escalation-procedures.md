# Chapter 18 — Escalation Procedures

Escalation is not a failure of the front-line team — it is a design feature of a well-run service organization. Knowing when and how to escalate protects the customer, the team, and the shop's reputation. This chapter defines the conditions that trigger escalation and the process for handling each.

## Escalation Triggers

| Trigger | Escalation Level | Immediate Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer is not satisfied with a resolution offered by the SA | CSM | SA introduces CSM within the same interaction if possible |
| Complaint involves potential vehicle damage caused by the shop | CSM + Lead Installer + Owner | Pull vehicle; photograph; do not release until assessed |
| Customer mentions legal action, attorney, or small claims | Owner + Legal Counsel | Do not discuss further — refer all communication to the owner |
| Customer posts a negative review during an active service dispute | CSM + Owner | CSM responds publicly within 2 hours; private resolution immediately |
| Customer arrives at the shop in a visibly distressed or aggressive state | CSM or Owner — immediately | Move to private space; do not engage publicly; involve owner if escalation continues |
| A quality failure is identified that may affect multiple vehicles from the same product batch | CSM + Lead Installer + Supplier | Contact supplier; identify affected vehicles; proactively contact customers |
| A team member makes a pricing or service promise they are not authorized to make | CSM | Review with team member privately; contact customer to clarify within 24 hours |

## How to Introduce an Escalation to a Customer

<div class="callout script">

"[Name], I want to make sure this gets handled at the highest level. Let me bring in [CSM/Owner Name], who has the authority to make sure we get this right for you. I'm going to step away for just a moment to brief them — I appreciate your patience."

</div>

## CSM Escalation Response Protocol

<div class="procedure">

### CSM Handling an Escalated Complaint

1. Review all available information in CRM before engaging the customer
2. Acknowledge the issue and the customer's frustration before offering any resolution
3. Conduct a physical inspection of the vehicle if it is on premises — never resolve sight-unseen if the vehicle is present
4. Provide a clear resolution within the authority level — do not make commitments that require owner approval before obtaining it
5. Document the conversation in CRM with every commitment made
6. Follow up personally within 24 hours of resolution to confirm the customer is satisfied
7. Conduct an internal post-mortem with the SA and lead installer to understand the root cause

</div>

## Documentation Requirements for Escalated Cases

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Full timeline of events from first contact through resolution</li>
<li>All communication records (calls, texts, emails)</li>
<li>Intake photos referenced against post-service photos</li>
<li>Names of all staff involved in the service</li>
<li>Resolution offered and customer acceptance / rejection</li>
<li>Any cost incurred by the shop in resolution (redo labor, material, refund, credit)</li>
<li>Final customer satisfaction assessment (verbal or written)</li>
<li>Process improvement recommendation if failure was systemic</li>
</ul>
