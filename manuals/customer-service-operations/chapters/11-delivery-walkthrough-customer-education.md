# Chapter 11 — Delivery Walkthrough & Customer Education

The delivery moment is the shop's opportunity to turn a satisfied customer into an enthusiastic ambassador. The delivery walkthrough should feel like an unveiling — structured, proud, and educational. It is also the last chance to confirm quality before the vehicle leaves the property.

## Pre-Delivery Checklist (Internal)

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Final QC inspection completed and signed off by the lead installer</li>
<li>Vehicle is cleaned — interior surfaces that were touched are wiped down; exterior is spot-free</li>
<li>Vehicle is staged in a clean, well-lit area for the walkthrough</li>
<li>All materials removed from interior (plastic, tape, tools)</li>
<li>Floor mats are returned to original positions</li>
<li>All keys are accounted for</li>
<li>Aftercare package (printed guide + product if applicable) is ready to hand over</li>
<li>Invoice is prepared and payment method is confirmed</li>
<li>Review request is prepared (link, QR code, or direct text)</li>
</ul>

## Delivery Walkthrough Script

<div class="procedure">

### Customer Delivery Walkthrough

1. **The Reveal:** Walk the customer to the vehicle with energy — "Wait until you see this." Allow a moment of reaction before talking.
2. **The Tour:** Walk the entire vehicle clockwise pointing out quality details: "Notice how clean the edge is here…", "Look at how the film wraps around the corner…"
3. **The Education:** Explain what was done, not just what the customer can see: "We prepped the surface with [process], applied [product], and here's what that means for you going forward."
4. **Demonstrate self-healing (PPF) or hydrophobic (ceramic) if applicable** — live demos build memory and excitement.
5. **Review the aftercare guide together** — highlight the top three rules and hand them the guide.
6. **Address any questions fully** — do not rush this moment.
7. **Collect payment** — present the invoice and process payment before returning the key.
8. **Return the key personally** — not through a window or left on a counter.
9. **The Ask:** "We'd love to know what you thought — would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really makes a difference for us." Hand them the QR code or link.
10. **The Close:** "It was our pleasure. You know where we are if you need anything — we're here for you." Handshake or genuine farewell.

</div>

## Delivery Moment Don'ts

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Do NOT have the customer pick up the vehicle before the walkaround is complete</li>
<li>Do NOT hand keys over a counter — walk them to the vehicle</li>
<li>Do NOT rush through aftercare because the customer seems busy</li>
<li>Do NOT apologize for the price at delivery — the job is done; own the value</li>
<li>Do NOT ask for a review in a way that sounds scripted or transactional</li>
<li>Do NOT leave any defect unaddressed — if you see it, the customer will see it</li>
</ul>

## Handling a Delivery Concern

On rare occasions, the customer will identify a concern during the walkthrough — a visual imperfection, an area they are unhappy with, or a question about whether something is right. These moments must be handled calmly and decisively.

<div class="procedure">

### Delivery Concern Protocol

1. Listen fully — let the customer finish describing the concern without interrupting
2. Look at it together — physically get close to what they are pointing at
3. Determine whether it is a defect, a limitation inherent to the service, or a pre-existing condition
4. If it is a true defect: "I want to make this right. Here's what I'd like to do…" — give a specific resolution
5. If it is a service limitation: Walk them through the expectation that was set and the sample/documentation
6. If it is pre-existing: Reference the intake form and photos
7. Never argue about whether it is a defect — focus on resolution
8. Log the concern and resolution in the CRM job record
9. If resolution requires a return appointment, schedule before the customer leaves

</div>
