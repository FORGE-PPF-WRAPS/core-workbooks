# Chapter 12 — New Car Delivery Preparation

New car delivery preparation is a high-value, time-sensitive service that gives the professional detailer access to vehicles at their most pristine state — before factory transport contamination, dealer lot dust, and showroom handling have had time to cause damage. Understanding the unique challenges of new vehicle prep positions the shop to serve dealers, private buyers taking delivery, and brand-enthusiast clients who want their new vehicle treated to the highest professional standard from day one.

## New Car Contamination Reality

A "new" vehicle arriving at a dealership or via transport truck is far from the pristine showroom condition the customer envisions. The typical new vehicle delivery condition includes:

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Transport dust and road contamination from the delivery trip (often 500–1,000+ km)</li>
<li>Overspray from surrounding vehicles during transport on an open carrier</li>
<li>Factory wax — a cheap protective wax applied at the factory that must be fully removed before any quality product is applied</li>
<li>Rail and iron fallout from rail transport — the single most contamination-heavy transport method</li>
<li>Adhesive tape residue from protective plastic sheeting removed at the dealership</li>
<li>Fingerprints and handling marks from PDI inspection at the dealer</li>
<li>Dealer showroom film — the light dust and contamination accumulated on the lot</li>
<li>In some cases: touch-up paint applied at the dealership to conceal transport damage — this must be identified and documented before any correction work</li>
</ul>

## New Car Prep Sequence

<div class="procedure">

### Complete New Car Preparation Protocol

1. **Visual inspection first** — photograph all four sides and roof before touching the vehicle; note any transport damage, touch-up paint, panel damage, or contamination
2. **Factory wax removal wash** — use a strip wash (not pH-neutral maintenance shampoo) at 50:1–100:1 dilution; hand wash with a clean mitt; thorough rinse
3. **Iron fallout removal** — new vehicles shipped by rail have extremely high iron contamination; apply iron remover to all panels; observe the reaction; rinse thoroughly
4. **Clay bar decontamination** — use a fine or medium clay; the surface should feel smooth and glass-like after this stage
5. **Paint depth measurement** — record baseline readings for all panels on a new vehicle; this establishes the factory-original thickness for future reference and protects both the shop and the customer against any future dispute
6. **Paint correction (if needed)** — assess under raking detail light; many new vehicles have holograms from the factory polishing line or dealership PDI buffing; correct to the appropriate standard
7. **IPA wipe all panels** — confirm all factory wax, transport wax, and polishing oils are removed
8. **Apply protection (per customer's selection)**: PPF, ceramic coating, paint sealant, or combination service

</div>
