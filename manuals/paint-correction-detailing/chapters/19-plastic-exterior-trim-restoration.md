# Chapter 19 — Plastic & Exterior Trim Restoration

Exterior plastic trim restoration addresses one of the most visible signs of vehicle aging: faded, gray, or dried-out black trim panels. Door trim strips, wheel arch cladding, body side moldings, and bumper trim can all be restored from a chalky gray appearance to a deep, rich black that dramatically improves the vehicle's overall appearance. This service pairs naturally with any detail package.

## Understanding Plastic Trim Degradation

Exterior plastic trim is typically made from unpainted black polypropylene or ABS plastic. Unlike clear-coated paint, these plastics are not protected by a lacquer layer. UV radiation attacks the surface plasticizers in the plastic, causing them to leach out — leaving a dry, chalky surface. Silicone-based products temporarily mask this by coating the surface, but do not replace lost plasticizers.

## Restoration Methods by Severity

| Severity | Appearance | Method | Expected Result |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Light fading | Slightly gray; still has some texture definition | Trim-specific restoration product (penetrating formula); wipe on; allow to penetrate; buff off | Full restoration; results last 12–24 months |
| Moderate fading | Gray-white; texture partly obscured by surface chalking | Light machine polish with a foam pad to remove surface chalk; apply penetrating restorer; seal with trim coating | Good restoration; sealing extends results to 2–3 years |
| Severe chalking | White surface layer; deeply dried; texture nearly invisible | Wet sand 1500-grit to remove chalked surface layer; machine polish; penetrating restorer; seal with trim ceramic or UV coating | Best achievable restoration; results without sealant will fade within months |
| Staining (paint overspray, tar) | Discolored but not necessarily faded | Overspray remover or clay bar on the trim surface; clean; apply restorer | Varies by stain type; paint overspray may require wet-sanding |

## Trim Restoration Procedure

<div class="procedure">

### Exterior Plastic Trim Restoration

1. Clean the trim with a dedicated trim cleaner or APC at 5:1 — remove all wax, silicone, and surface contamination; old silicone dressings prevent penetration of restoration products
2. If moderately or severely chalked: apply a light compound to a foam pad; machine polish at low speed (DA speed 3–4); this removes the chalked surface layer and prepares a fresh substrate for the restorer
3. Apply the penetrating trim restorer per product instructions — typically: apply to a foam applicator; work into the trim in circular motion; allow to penetrate for 5–10 minutes
4. Buff off any excess with a clean microfiber — the trim should be deep black, not shiny or oily-looking
5. If ceramic trim coating is being applied: IPA wipe the trim; apply the trim-specific ceramic coating to an applicator; wipe onto the trim in overlapping strokes; level per product instructions
6. Avoid getting any restorer or coating on painted surfaces — mask the paint edge if necessary
7. Allow to cure per product instructions before exposing to rain or washing

</div>

## Trim Restoration Products to Avoid

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Silicone-based tire shine or vinyl dressing applied to trim — temporary; slings off onto paint in driving; customer complaints of greasy paint</li>
<li>Petroleum-based products — degrades the plastic further over time</li>
<li>Paste wax on unpainted black trim — leaves a white residue in the texture channels that is extremely difficult to remove</li>
<li>Abrasive polish rated 3H or below — too aggressive for most trim plastics; can remove the surface texture permanently</li>
</ul>
