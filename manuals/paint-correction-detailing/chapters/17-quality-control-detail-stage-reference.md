# Chapter 17 — Quality Control & Detail Stage Reference

Quality control in detailing is a customer satisfaction driver at a direct, visible level — the customer can immediately evaluate the result when they inspect their vehicle. This chapter provides a structured QC framework for every detailing stage.

## Exterior QC Checklist

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Paint surface is completely free of water spots, soap residue, and wax haze</li>
<li>All chrome and brightwork is polished and streak-free</li>
<li>Rubber trim is dressed — matte and uniform, not shiny or over-saturated</li>
<li>Tires are clean and evenly dressed — no wipe marks or sling-off on the fenders</li>
<li>Wheels are clean on face, spokes, and barrel (verify barrel with a light)</li>
<li>Glass is clean and streak-free — check from inside and outside</li>
<li>Door jambs are clean and dressed — not forgotten or half-done</li>
<li>Fuel filler door surround is clean — this is a detail people notice immediately</li>
<li>All badging and trim is clean and free of compound or product residue</li>
<li>License plate area and frame are clean</li>
<li>Wiper blades are wiped — not streaking from product contamination</li>
</ul>

## Interior QC Checklist

<ul class="checklist">
<li>No dust visible on the dashboard, vents, or any horizontal surface</li>
<li>All glass is streak-free — check from the driver's seat and from outside</li>
<li>Leather is clean and conditioned — not dry; not greasy or over-conditioned</li>
<li>Carpets are uniformly clean and pile is lifted (not matted in one direction)</li>
<li>Floor mats are correctly positioned and secured</li>
<li>Center console is clean — including the inside of cup holders and the charging port area</li>
<li>All door panel pockets and armrests are clean</li>
<li>Trunk is vacuumed and any cargo area liner is dressed</li>
<li>Headliner is spot-free — particularly around map lights and sunroof edges</li>
<li>Odor is neutral or lightly fresh — no overwhelming fragrance; no lingering chemical smell</li>
</ul>

## Correction-Level QC

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Pre-correction photos are compared to post-correction inspection under the same light setup</li>
<li>No holograms from rotary polishing — inspect specifically at 45° angles under single-point light</li>
<li>No buffer trails (straight line marks from DA moving too fast) — inspect under raking LED</li>
<li>No compound splatter on rubber trim, glass, or panel edges</li>
<li>All polishing oils removed by IPA wipe — no oily sheen visible</li>
<li>Customer is shown the before-and-after comparison before delivery is confirmed</li>
</ul>

## QC Sign-Off System

No vehicle leaves the shop without a physical or digital QC sign-off. This is not optional — it is the single procedural step that prevents the majority of post-delivery complaints. The sign-off is performed by a person other than the technician who performed the service whenever possible.

| QC Stage | Who Signs Off | What Is Checked | What Happens If Issue Found |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Post-wash QC | Any senior technician | Surface is clean; no contamination; panels dry; no water spots | Re-wash or spot treat; do not proceed to correction until passed |
| Post-correction QC | Lead technician or shop manager | All panels inspected under inspection bar; defect map reviewed; holograms; induced marring | Re-correct the specific panel; do not allow the vehicle to proceed to coating until all panels pass |
| Post-coating QC | Lead technician or shop manager | All coated surfaces inspected; no high spots; no missed areas; hydrophobic test on spot panel | Rework high spots; add a second coat to missed areas; do not release the vehicle until passed |
| Delivery QC | Service advisor or front desk | Interior clean; no product residue on exterior; personal items returned; fuel level as received | Correct any cosmetic issues before the customer enters the vehicle |

## QC Documentation for Coating Jobs

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Before photos: minimum 20 photos of intake condition — all panels, all glass, wheels, pre-existing damage closeups</li>
<li>Defect map completed with condition grade and specific defect notes for each panel before correction begins</li>
<li>PTG readings recorded for each panel — minimum one reading per panel; three readings for any panel with unusual thickness</li>
<li>Post-correction photos: minimum 8 comparison photos showing the most significantly corrected areas</li>
<li>Coating application record: product name and lot number; ambient conditions; panels coated in sequence; flash and leveling notes; IR cure duration if used</li>
<li>Final inspection sign-off form: technician initial and manager countersign; all panels confirmed pass; delivery authorized</li>
<li>All documentation uploaded to the CRM before the customer is contacted for pickup — no verbal delivery without the paper trail in place</li>
</ul>

## Detail Stages Reference Section

The following stage cards provide a quick-reference overview of each detailing service stage defined in this manual.

### Stage 1 — Express Detail

| Attribute | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Duration | 60–90 minutes |
| Typical Price Range | $80–$150 |
| Key Notes | Express detail is a maintenance service — it does not include paint decontamination, correction, or coating. Do not promise a restored appearance from an express detail on a neglected vehicle. |

<div class="procedure">

### Stage 1 — Express Detail — Steps

1. Exterior hand wash (two-bucket method; pH-neutral soap)
2. Rinse and dry with microfiber drying towel
3. Glass cleaning inside and out with ammonia-free glass cleaner
4. Interior vacuum (seats, carpet, floor mats)
5. Interior wipe-down of hard surfaces with an all-purpose cleaner at appropriate dilution
6. Tire shine and wheel spray wipe
7. Spray detailer or quick wax on exterior for light protection

</div>

<div class="page-break"></div>

### Stage 2 — Standard Detail

| Attribute | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Duration | 3–5 hours |
| Typical Price Range | $200–$350 |
| Key Notes | The standard detail is the backbone of a detailing menu. It should leave the vehicle looking significantly better than when it arrived without the need for machine polishing. |

<div class="procedure">

### Stage 2 — Standard Detail — Steps

1. Pre-rinse to remove loose debris
2. Foam cannon application; 5-minute dwell
3. Two-bucket hand wash; panel by panel from roof down
4. Rinse; dry with microfiber
5. Clay bar decontamination on all painted surfaces
6. Iron fallout removal if indicated by inspection
7. Glass cleaning inside and out
8. Full interior vacuum and extraction of floor mats
9. Interior surface wipe with appropriate products for each material type
10. Leather conditioning (if leather interior)
11. Tire and wheel cleaning with dedicated products; tire dressing
12. Engine bay light cleaning if included in scope
13. Application of spray sealant or carnauba wax

</div>

<div class="page-break"></div>

### Stage 3 — Paint Correction Detail

| Attribute | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Duration | 1–2 days (depending on vehicle size and defect severity) |
| Typical Price Range | $500–$1,500+ |
| Key Notes | Paint correction detailing is a skilled service requiring machine polishing experience. Never assign this stage to a technician who has not completed supervised correction training. |

<div class="procedure">

### Stage 3 — Paint Correction Detail — Steps

1. Full Stage 2 decontamination wash and clay
2. Paint thickness measurement on all panels
3. Paint condition assessment under LED detail light; photograph all defects
4. Masking of trim, rubber, and glass as needed
5. Machine polish — compound stage: correct scratches, swirls, and oxidation
6. Machine polish — finishing stage: refine surface to high gloss
7. IPA panel wipe after polishing to remove polish oils
8. Inspect each panel under raking detail light; re-polish any areas not at standard
9. Application of carnauba wax, sealant, or handover for ceramic coating application
10. Interior detail concurrent with paint work (Stage 2 interior scope)
11. Glass decontamination and treatment
12. Final vehicle inspection and QC

</div>

<div class="page-break"></div>

### Stage 4 — Show Preparation Detail

| Attribute | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Duration | 2–4 days |
| Typical Price Range | $1,500–$4,000+ |
| Key Notes | Show preparation is a premium service reserved for concours competitors, collectors, and enthusiasts preparing for an event. Every minute detail is addressed. Price reflects the time, skill, and materials involved. |

<div class="procedure">

### Stage 4 — Show Preparation Detail — Steps

1. All Stage 3 steps completed to 95%+ defect removal standard
2. Final jeweling stage with ultra-fine finishing compound (20,000+ grit equivalent)
3. Panel-by-panel inspection under high-intensity show lighting
4. Spot correction on any remaining micro-defect not acceptable under show inspection
5. Elite-grade paint coating or carnauba wax application
6. Wheel removal; full wheel and brake caliper detail and coating
7. Engine bay show preparation: detail, dress, and protect all engine bay surfaces
8. Interior deep clean and leather conditioning; dress all plastics and trim
9. Tires cleaned and dressed with water-based tire product (not silicone-based)
10. Final inspection with the customer present

</div>
