## 3. Authorized use

You may use Marks only if:

<ul class="checklist">
<li>You have written authorization (employment, license, partner agreement, or brand guidelines)</li>
<li>Use follows the Brand Style Guide (colors, clear space, minimum size)</li>
<li>Use is truthful and does not imply endorsement beyond your actual relationship</li>
<li>Marks are not altered, distorted, or combined with other elements without approval</li>
</ul>

---

## 4. Prohibited use

Without prior written consent, you may **not**:

<ul class="checklist">
<li>Use Marks as part of your own company name or domain name</li>
<li>Use Marks on products or services not provided or approved by Company</li>
<li>Use Marks in comparative advertising that disparages Company</li>
<li>Register Marks or confusingly similar marks with any registry</li>
<li>Remove trademark symbols (™, ®) where required by style guide</li>
<li>Use Marks after termination of your agreement with Company</li>
</ul>
