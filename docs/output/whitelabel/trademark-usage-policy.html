3. Authorized use

You may use Marks only if:

  • You have written authorization (employment, license, partner agreement, or brand guidelines)
  • Use follows the Brand Style Guide (colors, clear space, minimum size)
  • Use is truthful and does not imply endorsement beyond your actual relationship
  • Marks are not altered, distorted, or combined with other elements without approval

4. Prohibited use

Without prior written consent, you may not:

  • Use Marks as part of your own company name or domain name
  • Use Marks on products or services not provided or approved by Company
  • Use Marks in comparative advertising that disparages Company
  • Register Marks or confusingly similar marks with any registry
  • Remove trademark symbols (™, ®) where required by style guide
  • Use Marks after termination of your agreement with Company

5. Co-branding & white-label

Licensed partners using white-label materials must follow the White-Label Agreement and submit samples for approval before public distribution.

Referral partners may use approved co-branded assets only — not standalone Marks as if partner is Company.
